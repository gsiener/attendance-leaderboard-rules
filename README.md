# Attendance Leaderboard

A point-based system for tracking and rewarding attendance at practices and games. The goal is simple: **show up, and if you can't, let us know.**

## How It Works

Every practice and game is worth points. Points accumulate over the season. The leaderboard ranks players by total points.

## Point Values

| Scenario | Points |
|---|---|
| Attend, on time | 1.0 |
| RSVP bonus (confirmed attendance in advance) | +0.2 |
| Early arrival bonus (before start time) | +0.1 |
| Buddy bonus (you and a buddy both attend) | +0.2 |
| Attend, late (15+ minutes) | 0.7 |
| Absent, notified in advance | 0.5 |
| Absent, no notice | 0.0 |

Bonuses stack. A player who RSVPs, arrives early, and whose buddy is also there earns **1.5 points**.

## Definitions

**On time**: Arrive by the scheduled start time.

**Early**: Cleats on and ready to go before the scheduled start time. This earns a +0.1 bonus on top of the standard 1.0.

**RSVP'd**: Confirmed you'd attend with a coach before the event — and then showed up. This earns a +0.2 bonus whether you arrive early, on time, or late. RSVPing "no" counts the same as notifying in advance (0.5).

**Buddy bonus**: Each player is assigned one or two buddies by the coaches. When you and a buddy both attend the same event, you each earn +0.2. The bonus is per event, not per buddy — attending with one or both buddies is the same +0.2.

**Late**: Arrive 15 or more minutes after the scheduled start time. You still get credit for showing up, but at a reduced rate.

**Notified in advance**: Let a coach know you won't be at practice *before* the scheduled start time. Any notice before practice starts counts — text, message, or in person.

**No notice**: You didn't show up and didn't tell anyone beforehand.

## Rules

1. **Games and practices are scored the same.** Every scheduled team event counts equally.
2. **Points are cumulative.** Your leaderboard position is based on your total points across the entire season.
3. **Coaches determine arrival status.** If there's a question about whether you were on time or late, the coaches' call is final.
4. **Notification must reach a coach.** Telling a teammate doesn't count — make sure a coach sees your message.

## Season

The current season runs from **February 28, 2026** through **NY State Championships (Memorial Day weekend, May 23–25, 2026)**.

## Leaderboard Updates

The leaderboard is updated at the coaches' discretion and may be updated after any practice or game.

## Why This System?

- **Showing up is the most important thing.** That's why it's worth a full point.
- **Being early matters.** It shows commitment and gives the team more time together.
- **RSVPing matters.** Knowing who's coming helps coaches plan. Confirming in advance earns a bonus whether you're early, on time, or even late.
- **Buddies keep each other accountable.** You earn more when your buddy shows up too — so check in on each other.
- **Communication matters.** Life happens — we get it. But letting us know you can't make it shows respect for the team. That's worth something (0.5), even if it's not the same as being there.
- **No-shows with no notice earn nothing.** The team can't plan around you if you don't communicate.
